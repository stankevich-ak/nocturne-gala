Rehearsals

Five rehearsals before the ball.

Guests did not arrive cold. Before the Spring Gala, we met for five guided rehearsals and built the evening step by step, so the final night felt social instead of intimidating.

We focused on accessible historic dances, partner changes, group formations, and enough repetition for the room to feel familiar by the time the ball began.

  • Group dances
  • Polka for Three and Spanish Waltz
  • Ceremonial Polonaise
  • Couple Waltz
  • Rotational partner Minuet and Cotillons

The Ball

Dancing, live performers, music, and a full spring table.

The final evening brought the rehearsals into a decorated room: guests danced together, performers joined the program, and the music carried the night from the opening dances into the final social moments.

There was a Queen and King competition, a generous food table, and the kind of room where people kept finding new partners, familiar faces, and another reason to stay.

  • Live performers and music throughout the evening
  • Queen and King competition
  • Food, refreshments, and social time
  • Beginner-friendly dances from rehearsal to final night
